Carmina Burana Online

The Codex Buranus (Munich, Bayerische Staatsbibliothek, Clm 4660–4660a), copied in the thirteenth century, is a major witness to medieval poetry and music. It preserves the richest surviving anthology of Latin lyric poetry, the earliest source of Minnesang, and a remarkable collection of liturgical plays.

This website provides access to a digital edition of the Carmina Burana, based on the Codex Buranus and more than 400 other medieval manuscripts. Developed within the framework of the SNSF project Carmina Burana Online (2024–2027) at the Schola Cantorum Basiliensis / FHNW, this edition brings together texts, melodies, translations, critical commentaries, and source images.

Particular attention is devoted to the sonic dimension of Middle Latin rhythmic poetry and Middle High German lyric. The project explores and reconstructs the melodies preserved in around sixty sources, ranging from the staffless neumes of the Codex Buranus to the mensural notations of the late Middle Ages.

The website will also offer new performance-based approaches to a selection of pieces, as well as audio and video recordings produced at the Schola Cantorum Basiliensis.

At the intersection of scholarship, digital humanities, and musical practice, Carmina Burana Online invites scholars, musicians, and a wider public to rediscover the sound world of medieval poetry.
